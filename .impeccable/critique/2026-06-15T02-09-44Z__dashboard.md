## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**Does this look AI-generated?**

**LLM assessment:** Partially. There are genuine domain-specific decisions here — the inline waterfall calculator, the ACQ/STAB grade system, the cashback-at-close editing — that signal real product thinking. But the surface language is template-grade. Any designer familiar with dark dashboard kits would correctly identify the aesthetic family on sight: *dark background + gold accent + glass cards + uppercase eyebrow labels + big number metrics*. The institutional brand is aspirational; it has not yet been achieved in the implementation.

Specific tells found:
- **Hero-metric template (banned):** `achievement-board.tsx` lines 236–277: four identical `MetricCard` components, icon + `num-hero` class + `label-eyebrow` + `metric-accent::after` gold underline. `dashboard-intel.tsx` lines 195–234: same structure under the class name `KpiCard`. Two independent implementations of the same forbidden pattern appear sequentially on the same page.
- **Identical card grids (banned):** Two separate 4-up card grids (`grid-cols-2 lg:grid-cols-4`) stack within ~300px of each other on the dashboard home.
- **Label-eyebrow on everything (banned):** `.label-eyebrow` (`0.6rem, uppercase, letter-spacing: 0.16em`) applied to section titles, card field labels, panel headers, filter labels, chart tab labels, leaderboard headers, "Quick Links" — 20+ instances across the codebase. It is the default fallback for any text that isn't a number.
- **Glassmorphism as default (banned):** `.glass-card` applied to every card surface across all views. `backdrop-filter: blur(12px)` + diagonal gradient is the standard container, not a purposeful depth signal.
- **Side-stripe accent (banned):** `pipeline-board.tsx` line 760: `borderLeft: '3px solid ${leftBorder}'` on deal cards using `STATUS_LEFT_BORDER` color map. `sidebar.tsx` line 86: `border-l-2 border-[#C9A84C]` on active nav item.
- **Gradient text:** Not found. One gradient background on a tab button is within tolerance.

## Overall Impression

The pipeline board and deal panel are genuinely good — dense, data-forward, institutionally credible. The waterfall calculator is a real product differentiator. Everything surrounding them is generic dark-dashboard scaffolding that undercuts the brand. The most urgent technical problem is invisible: `--font-display` is pointing at DM Sans, which means Cormorant Garamond — the entire typographic identity — has never rendered correctly. Fix that first, then address the dashboard IA: this product opens with an achievement board when it should open with the operator's pipeline.

## What's Working

**1. The pipeline deal card is the best surface in the product.**
Three-column data layout (Purchase / Cashback% / PAI Fee), grade pills + status badge, rental toggle, `whileHover: { y: -2 }` lift with gold border glow. Restrained and correct. Would feel at home in an institutional tool.

**2. Inline waterfall calculator with live preview is a genuine product insight.**
`OverviewTab` in `pipeline-board.tsx` tracks 10 waterfall inputs and recalculates the Morby waterfall in real time. DPTS and Est. Net to Buyer update as the operator types. This is the right model for deal evaluation — no competitor does this inline at this level of fidelity.

**3. The ACQ/STAB grade badge system is semantically coherent.**
A/B/C/D/F with threshold-based colors appears consistently across pipeline cards, portfolio client, and deal panels. The `dim` state for ungraded deals handles the null case without noise. Domain-meaningful, consistently applied.

## Priority Issues

### [P1] Font-display CSS variable wired to wrong font
**File:** `src/app/globals.css` line 64
**What:** `--font-display: 'DM Sans', sans-serif`. This should be Cormorant Garamond. Every `h1`, `h2`, `.num-hero`, `.num-metric`, `.text-display`, `.text-metric-lg`, `.text-metric-md` in the product resolves to DM Sans. Cormorant Garamond — the serif face meant to carry the institutional register — has never rendered in production because the CSS variable is wrong.
**Why it matters:** The typographic identity of the product is broken at the root. The Precise · Private · Institutional brand is half-built on type contrast (humanist sans + classical serif). Both the sans and the serif are currently rendering as the same font. The `layout.tsx` loads Cormorant Garamond and assigns it to `--font-display`, but globals.css overwrites the variable in `:root`, which takes precedence.
**Fix:** Remove line 64 from globals.css. The font variable is already set correctly by `layout.tsx` via Next.js font injection into `<html>`. The `:root` override in globals.css is shadowing it.
**Suggested command:** `/impeccable typeset`

### [P1] Dashboard home page serves no decision
**File:** `src/app/dashboard/page.tsx`
**What:** The operator's first view is the Achievement Board — historical deal counts, total cashback, fees paid, leaderboard. Pipeline health (the primary operational data) is in `DashboardIntel`, which renders below the Achievement Board. An operator opening mid-deal must scroll past four achievement cards, a leaderboard, a trend chart, a deal mix chart, 4 KPI cards, and 2 pipeline panels before reaching anything actionable.
**Why it matters:** Breaks "Operator cadence" — the first principle is that operators open the app to make decisions, not to review history. Time-to-action for "run underwriting on a pending deal" is 8 steps minimum from the home page.
**Fix:** Lead with `DashboardIntel` (the live pipeline panels). Push `AchievementBoard` below the fold or onto a dedicated "Reports" view. Consider a single attention-directing element at the top: "3 deals in escrow · 2 pending review" as the opening beat.
**Suggested command:** `/impeccable shape dashboard`

### [P1] Auto-save on blur with no confirmation on financial data
**Files:** `dashboard-intel.tsx` line 309; `pipeline-board.tsx` line 998
**What:** Both cashback input fields save on blur — tab navigation, a click outside, a browser autofill event all trigger a silent PATCH to the database. No confirmation, no undo, no success/failure feedback. Errors are swallowed silently.
**Why it matters:** An operator mistyping a cashback % or clicking outside while editing can corrupt the deal's financial model with no way to know it happened and no way to recover. For a financial instrument this is a P1 error prevention failure.
**Fix:** Add debounced explicit save (Enter key or Save button), or at minimum add an undo toast ("Cashback updated to X% — undo") via Sonner that fires on every save with a 5s window.
**Suggested command:** `/impeccable harden`

### [P2] Two simultaneous gold tokens with no semantic distinction
**Files:** `globals.css` lines 34–35 vs. 63; `impact-board.tsx` line 123; `portfolio-client.tsx` lines 132, 287, 321
**What:** `--gold: #d4af37` and `--text-gold: #C9A84C` are both in active use. `impact-board.tsx` uses `text-[#d4af37]` directly. Both golds appear on the same page in adjacent elements with a visible hue/value difference (~15 delta-E — definitely perceptible).
**Why it matters:** Breaks "Institutional weight" and "Precise." Two slightly-different golds read as a mistake, not a decision. For a brand whose entire accent identity rests on one color, two is one too many.
**Fix:** Audit all gold usages. Canonicalize on `#C9A84C` (the warmer, darker tone already used in the dark-theme surfaces). Replace all `#d4af37`, `#D4AF37`, `#C9A84C`-adjacent values with the single token `--text-gold`.
**Suggested command:** `/impeccable polish`

### [P2] `.label-eyebrow` is structural scaffolding, not a design decision
**Files:** `achievement-board.tsx`, `dashboard-intel.tsx`, `portfolio-client.tsx`, `pipeline-board.tsx`, `impact-board.tsx`
**What:** The eyebrow class (`0.6rem, uppercase, tracking: 0.16em`) is applied to section titles, card field labels, panel headers, filter labels, chart tab labels, leaderboard section names, and "Quick Links" — 20+ instances. It is the universal default for "text that isn't a number." At 0.6rem it is near-illegible on standard displays.
**Why it matters:** Every section having the same eyebrow is the banned pattern — "Tiny uppercase tracked eyebrow above every section." It reads as template scaffold, not type hierarchy. It also competes for the same visual register as labels that need to be distinctly readable (field labels in the deal panel, for example).
**Fix:** Reserve `.label-eyebrow` for one purpose: section-level grouping headers (e.g., "Pipeline" above a set of deal rows). Use `.metric-label` or DM Sans at 0.75rem for field labels. Use a distinct weight/size for panel and tab headers.
**Suggested command:** `/impeccable typeset`

## Persona Red Flags

### Alex (Power User — Dashboard/Admin)
- Keyboard navigation: Escape to close deal panel only. Cannot open panels, navigate tabs, trigger underwriting, or move deals without a mouse.
- No bulk operations: To update cashback on 5 escrow deals, Alex must individually click each row.
- Filter chips (`pipeline-board.tsx` lines 293–303) look passive/decorative — could easily be missed by a user scanning for functional controls.
- `IntelligenceBar` collapse state not persisted: resets to open on every session.
- Count-up animations fire on every load even when data is unchanged, creating noise for a user who opens this app 10+ times per day.

### Sam (Accessibility-Dependent)
- `EscrowRow` cashback input (`dashboard-intel.tsx` lines 300–314): no `aria-label`, only a `$` prefix span as context.
- `DealPanel` backdrop (`pipeline-board.tsx` lines 330–367): `<div>` overlay with no `role="dialog"`, no `aria-modal`, no focus trap. Opening the panel doesn't move focus inside it.
- Color-only state communication: `StatusBadge` and `GradeBadge` convey all status through color with no icon or text backup.
- `MiniStat` labels at `text-[9px]` in `pipeline-board.tsx` line 177: below WCAG 1.4.4 minimum.
- `EditRow` in `portfolio-client.tsx` (lines 1234–1276): edit trigger button has `title="Click to edit"` but no `aria-label`; role change on edit mode is not announced.

### The Operator (Creative Finance Investor, Deal Evaluation Mode)
- Lands on Achievement Board (history). Pipeline health is below the fold.
- Pipeline panels in `DashboardIntel` show truncated address rows (`text-xs truncate`) with no expand affordance.
- Cashback inputs in the escrow panel have no affordance signaling they're editable until hover.
- "Buybox Score" in the KPI strip and "Buybox Health" in the Portfolio escrow tab are different labels for the same metric. Operator must map these mentally.
- Time-to-underwriting from dashboard home: 8 steps minimum.

## Minor Observations

1. `ImpactBoard` and `AchievementBoard` both show a "Top Wholesalers" leaderboard. No clear hierarchy or deduplication between them.
2. `impact-board.tsx` has a visually separate design language (navy `#0f1c3f`, no glass-card, different type scale) — appears from an earlier iteration and was never reconciled with the current system.
3. Sidebar auto-collapses when a deal is closed and auto-expands after 4 seconds (`sidebar.tsx` lines 33–39). Hijacks the layout without user intent; causes visible layout shift.
4. Portfolio `StatsBar` (5 metrics) and immediately below it `IntelDashboard` showing 4 of the same 5 metrics. Direct duplication within the same page.
5. Celebrate banner in `portfolio-client.tsx` (lines 193–199) uses a 🎉 emoji — breaks the Precise · Private · Institutional register.
6. `AddHoldingModal` uses `bg-[#0a1628]` — a one-off navy hardcode not in the token system.
7. ~30 inline `style={{ fontFamily: 'var(--font-mono), monospace' }}` declarations in `pipeline-board.tsx` instead of using the `.font-mono` class from `globals.css`.
8. Four different money formatting functions (`fmtCompact`, `compactMoney`, `fmt$`, `moneyCompact`) across the codebase. A shared `moneyCompact` already exists in `@/lib/format`.
9. Three separate `useCountUp` implementations: one in `achievement-board.tsx`, one in `dashboard-intel.tsx`, and a shared one at `@/hooks/useCountUp` (already imported in `portfolio-client.tsx`).
10. TopBar consumes 56px of vertical height displaying only a user avatar. On a 900px laptop display, this is a significant cost for zero functional content.
11. Sidebar active item uses `border-l-2 border-[#C9A84C] pl-[10px]` — padding compensates for border width, creating a 2px horizontal jitter between active and inactive states.
12. `--ease-spring: cubic-bezier(0.34, 1.56, 0.64, 1)` in `globals.css` line 70 is an overshoot (bounce) curve. Applied on `glass-card:active`. Bounce is banned by the motion rules.
13. `.sidebar-collapsed { transition: width }` in `globals.css` line 338 animates a layout property, causing layout reflow on every sidebar toggle. Replace with `transform: translateX(-100%)` or `max-width`.

## Questions to Consider

**1. Why does the dashboard open with what happened, not what's happening?**
The Achievement Board shows closed deals and historical cashback — weekly-review-meeting data. The operator's most time-sensitive information (what's in escrow, what needs attention today) is the second section. If "Operator cadence" is a design principle, why is the operator forced to scroll past a leaderboard to reach the pipeline? What would the dashboard look like if it opened with one question: "What needs your attention right now?"

**2. The font-display variable has been wrong since initial setup — does the current product look the way anyone intended it to look?**
`--font-display: 'DM Sans'` in globals.css overwrites the Cormorant Garamond variable set by Next.js font injection. Every heading, hero metric, and display number in the product is rendering in the body font. The serif/sans type contrast — the primary carrier of the institutional register — has never been active in production. Is there a design comp anywhere showing what the product is supposed to look like with both fonts working?

**3. Does the pipeline deal card's left-border actually communicate status, or is it redundant signal that could be cut?**
`STATUS_LEFT_BORDER` assigns colors to deal statuses (pending/active/closed/dead). But status is also encoded by the `StatusBadge`, the grade pills, and the days-in-escrow counter. The left-border is one of the absolute bans (side-stripe accent). If it carries no unique information that isn't already present elsewhere on the card, cutting it would reduce visual noise and remove a design tell — while losing nothing.
